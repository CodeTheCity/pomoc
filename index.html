Access to health in your community

Healthcare is very different in the UK compared to Poland, here's what you need to know:
Have a cold, mild fever, minor aches and pains, or maybe some skin complaints? Your local pharmacist can sell you some remedies for this over the counter without a prescription and give you free advice on your condition.
If you find that your condition requires you to attend your doctor's surgery, the receptionist may ask you some questions about your condition. This is to direct you to the most appropriate person for your condition. This may not be a doctor, but may in stead be a nurse-practitioner, a practice nurse, or other health professional; this is to ensure that everyone can get an appointment suitable to their condition.

Pharmacy

Why would you go the pharmacy?

In Poland you would only go to the pharmacy to get a prescription fulfilled whereas in Scotland there are a greater range of services available.
The pharmacy is an accessible health resource for you and your family. Everyone can get advice from a pharmacist and buy medicine for a minor ailment or illness.
Many pharmacies are open when your GP surgery is shut (evenings, weekends and on public holidays). You don't even need to make an appointment to speak to a pharmacist.
A pharmacist provides lots of services and can help if:

You or a member of your family are suffering from a minor ailment or illness


There is a service, available from every pharmacy in Scotland, called the NHS Minor Ailment service.
Minor ailments or illnesses, which can be treated include:
  • Acne, athlete's foot, allergies.
  • Backache.
  • Coldsores, constipation, coughs.
  • Diarrhoea.
  • Earache, eczema.
  • Haemorrhoids (piles), hay fever, headache, head lice.
  • Indigestion.
  • Mouth ulcers.
  • Nasal congestion.
  • Pain, period pain.
  • Sore throat, thrush, threadworms.
  • Warts and verrucas.
  • Colds.
  • Eye infections.
  • Travel sickness.
  • Teething advice.
  • Worms.
Even if you are not eligible for this service the pharmacist will be able to provide you with advice on any of the ailments/illnesses listed above.
If the pharmacist is not able to help, your GP is closed, and they feel they can refer you to an out of hours doctor without you having to call NHS 24.
You can find out more about the NHS Minor Ailment Service on the Scottish Government website.
If you or a member of your family has run out of their regular repeat medication and your GP surgery is closed, and you are registered with a GP in Scotland, any community pharmacist can in certain circumstances under the NHS provide you with a supply of your regular repeat medication. This is at the discretion of the pharmacist and should only be in one off situations.
There are some medicines that the pharmacist is not allowed to supply and this service can not be used to obtain a supply of methadone or any other substitution therapy.
If you are on holiday from another part of the UK, or your regular pharmacy is closed a pharmacist can still make an emergency supply of your usual medicines.
If you are visiting from overseas, or you are not registered with a GP in the UK you need to contact a local surgery. You can find your nearest GP on this site or call NHS inform on 0800 22 44 88 (8am to 10pm, 7 days) However, you should remember:
  • To make sure you order and collect your repeat prescription in plenty of time so you don’t run out!
  • Remember that all GP surgeries are shut from 6pm on Friday night until 8am on Monday morning. GP surgeries are also closed on public holidays. Make sure you find out when your local public holidays are (these vary across Scotland) and when your GP will be open.
  • Not to phone NHS 24 if you have run out of your medicine, please call or visit a pharmacy first. You can find your nearest pharmacy on this site, or call NHS inform on 0800 22 44 88.
Pharmacists are experts about medicines, so any questions you have about your medicines and prescriptions can be answered by a pharmacist.

Doctor

Doctor services in Scotland are very different to those in Poland. Typical services offered can include:

Adult immunisation

All adults should be vaccinated against Tetanus / Diptheria. A Practice Nurse can advise on course of vaccinations.

Antenatal clinic

Antenatal clinics check pre-birth development and offer a range of advice

Child health clinic

This is to assess normal child development

Asthma clinic

This clinic is often run Practice Asthma Nurses and overseen by a Doctor

Cardiac rehabilitation clinic

This clinic is provided for patients who have suffered an MI or other cardiac problems. The Clinic is normally run by a Practice Nurse and overseen by a Doctor.

Child immunisation clinic

Advice and immunisations normally with the Practice Nurse.

Diabetic clinic

This clinic is normally run by a Practice Nurse.

District nurses, midwives & health visitors

District Nurses provide specialist nursing care and advice to patients in the community. Midwives are involved in all aspects of antenatal / postnatal care. Health Visitors are specialist nurses who have responsibility for child health, health promotion, etc in the community.

Epilepsy clinic

A Practice Nurse runs the clinic. Patients have an annual follow-up assessment and patient support given.

Family planning clinic

Each GP provides a full range of contraceptive services.

Home visits

Whenever possible try to go to the Surgery. If babies and children are unwell they would normally be seen more quickly by attending the Surgery. If it is cold, they can be wrapped up well and brought to the Surgery quite safely. Parents who have more than one child can usually contact a friend or relative willing to look after the rest of the family allowing them to attend the Surgery with the unwell child.

Hypertension clinic

This clinic is normally run by the Practice Hypertension Nurse. Patients blood pressure and medication will be assessed on a regular basis. Routine ECG and annual blood tests are performed to check cholesterol levels.

Non NHS services

If you need a medical examination or report, eg fitness to undertake sports, pre-employment, HGV, PSV or elderly driver, this will be carried out by appointment with the doctor. A fee will normally be charged.

Other services

Numerous other agencies and specialists hold clinics. These can include:

  • Clinical Psychologist
  • Dietician
  • CPN
  • Substance Misuse Team
  • Consultant Psychiatrist
Referral to these services is normally by your GP only.

Practice Nurse clinics

These are the main clinics run by Practice Nurses. The Practice Nurses are available by appointment only. They can help you with minor injuries, dressings, injections, smears, removal of stitches, ear syringing and blood tests. They can also advise on travel immunisations and a wide variety of health issues, but will refer you to the doctor if your condition requires it.

Practice Pharmacist

A Practice Pharmacist works closely with doctors and dispensary staff. As well as monitoring acute and repeat prescriptions she holds a weekly Hypertension Clinic and Ischaemic Heart Disease Clinic.

Preventative Services:

Provide information and advice on a variety of preventative services including:

  • Flu vaccination
  • Smoking cessation advice
  • Diet and Exercise.
Reception staff may be able to help you with advice on these matters or they may advise that you make an appointment with a nurse or a doctor to discuss these.

Stroke / TIA clinic

This clinic is run by a Practice Nurse overseen by a Doctor.

Travel clinic

Provides full travel and immunisation advice.
